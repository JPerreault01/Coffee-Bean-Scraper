# Technical Vocabulary

*Coffee terms as the community uses them. Reach for these instead of inventing phrasing.*

**1:2 ratio**: A brew ratio of 1 part coffee to 2 parts espresso output by weight; the reviewer targets this ratio for the shot.

**7:1 ratio**: A coffee-to-water ratio used in this recipe: 21 g of coffee to approximately 142 g of total water (42 g bloom + 100 g remaining), yielding an espresso-strength concentrate.

**a-roll / b-roll**: The creator mentions filming 'A and B roll' as part of his video production workflow, distinguishing primary talking-head footage from supplementary footage.

**abaca**: A plant-based fiber material used by Cafec as an alternative to standard paper pulp in one of their V60 filter papers, cited as having a sustainability benefit.

**acaia horizon**: A scale device used during the brew session; also appears to have a vacuum or suction feature being tested on the coffee cup, with adjustable settings (e.g., -0.5 bar).

**acidity**: A flavor dimension; Italian espresso is described as deliberately low in acidity through roasting/blending choices, contrasted with specialty coffee which 'celebrates' acidity.

**activated carbon filtration**: A filtration method using an activated carbon filter to remove chlorine, chloramine, VOCs, and off-tastes; described as core to high-quality filtration systems.

**aeropress**: A manual coffee brewing device; the central brewing tool of the post. The author describes assembling, loading, and pressing it as part of a brewing routine.

**aeropress filter**: Standard paper or metal filters that fit the AeroPress; the Premium uses the same filters despite its narrower chamber diameter.

**agitation**: Stirring the grounds 5–10 times after the main pour to promote even saturation and extraction.

**airlock**: A situation where trapped air prevents liquid from flowing freely through the brewer; hosts note the GX2's siphon mostly prevents this, with only negligible occurrences.

**alcoholic-fermentation natural**: Used to describe Finca Las Palmas' coffee — a natural-process coffee fermented using an alcoholic (likely anaerobic) fermentation method.

**algorithm**: Referenced in the context of YouTube's content distribution system, which the creator says drives a culture of chasing trends and growth metrics.

**alkalinity**: A water chemistry property; lower alkalinity allows acidity to stand out, while higher alkalinity (achieved e.g. with calcium carbonate) produces a creamier mouthfeel and buffers acidity.

**anaerobic**: A fermentation/processing method; used to describe the anaerobic coffee with 'Trix cereal' notes and the Nestor Lasso varietal set (anaerobic washed). The author notes anaerobics can carry 'funkiness' but this example did not.

**anaerobic fermentation**: Extended oxygen-restricted fermentation used to build complexity and sweetness; the source notes it can enhance complexity without overwhelming the cup when done with care.

**anaerobic natural**: A processing method where coffee ferments in a low-oxygen environment before drying; used here in two variants—9-day and 13-day—to describe progressively more intense fermentation profiles.

**anaerobic washed**: The specific processing method used for all four Nestor Lasso varietals in the Colombian set.

**arabica**: Higher-altitude coffee species that is largely unsuitable for Ghana's growing conditions; historically grown in small quantities in Ghanaian mountains but abandoned due to climate, elevation, insect infestation, and disease.

**arrival coffee**: The actual coffee received after purchase, as distinct from the sample material used to make the buying decision. Rao flags the risk that arrival coffee may be of lower quality than the sample.

**ast (authorized sca trainer)**: A credential held by Andre Eiermann, used to establish his authority on barista training.

**astringency**: A textural/taste defect the source references when cautioning about higher brew temperatures — higher temps may increase astringency for sensitive tasters.

**auction lot coffee**: A coffee sourced from a competitive auction, implying exceptional quality and traceability; Agua Catio is described as 'proper auction lot coffee.'

**auto-tare**: A feature in espresso mode that automatically zeros the scale without manual input, streamlining the workflow.

**automatic temperature control**: A feature on the Victoria Arduino Black Eagle espresso machine cited as an aid to temperature consistency.

**ba/kiki framework**: A flavor-profiling concept borrowed from psychology/linguistics: 'ba' = round, sweet, balanced, harmonious; 'kiki' = sharp, intense, unpredictable — used by the host to map dripper choice to desired cup character.

**backward compatibility**: MK3 and MK4 share all accessories and offer a familiar user experience across generations.

**ball bearings**: Bearings used to stabilize the axle in the standard barrel-style manual grinder design, considered a key structural feature of modern manual grinders.

**bar (pressure)**: A unit of pressure used to describe espresso brewing; pre-WWII machines operated at 1.5–2 bar; modern machines use 8–10 bar (with nine bar as the traditional standard per illycaffè).

**baratza encore**: A specific burr grinder referenced as the equipment for this recipe, with a recommended setting of 12–13 for medium-fine grind.

**barista express**: Used as the product name for the Breville Barista Express, an espresso machine with an integrated grinder, referenced here in the context of a retail purchase.

**basket funnel**: The standard moka pot funnel that sits in the boiler to hold ground coffee; retained on this modified device.

**basket plus kit**: An optional accessory for the Audin Nano (~$40–50 USD) that replaces the stock 6g pressurized basket with a larger, non-pressurized basket capable of holding 16–18g of coffee.

**baskets**: The portafilter basket that holds the coffee grounds; the poster references 'smaller baskets' requiring grind setting changes compared to their previous setup.

**batch brew**: Used in the context of the Orea Big Boy for group brewing — larger-volume brews served to multiple people at once.

**batch brew / drip coffee**: Filter coffee made in large quantities, typically from an urn; described as cheap and undervalued in the early 2000s.

**battery cycle**: Used interchangeably with brew cycle in the context of measuring how many shots can be pulled per full battery charge.

**bayonet closure**: A type of top-locking mechanism used on the kotyogó instead of the screw top found on traditional moka pots.

**bean-to-cup**: A style of automated coffee machine that handles grinding and brewing in one unit; the Magnolia was intended to be this type of machine.

**bed**: The layer of ground coffee in the dripper; getting the spout close to the bed is highlighted as a design advantage of the Origami Pin.

**bed depth**: Used to explain why 15 g is a good dose for the Z1 — it creates an appropriate balance between bed depth and the brewer's flow speed.

**blade grinder**: A grinder that uses spinning blades; grind size is controlled by how long the blades spin rather than by an adjustable gap.

**blend**: Used broadly across roasters (Onyx, Madcap, Olympia, Portland Coffee Roasters, Stumptown, Phil & Sebastian, Presta, Verve, Caffe Umbria) to describe coffees combining beans from multiple origins or processes.

**blend ratio**: The proportional contribution of each component in a blend (e.g., 3:2:1); Rao recommends no single component fall below 15% of the total blend to ensure dose-to-dose consistency.

**blending**: The practice of combining different coffees; cited alongside roasting as the key Italian technique for reducing acidity while maintaining high strength.

**blind cupping**: A cupping conducted without knowledge of which coffee is in which bowl, used by Prodigal to reduce evaluator bias.

**bloom**: A pre-infusion stage at the start of brewing during which low pressure is applied and the coffee slowly drips; the Morning Broom and Brew recipe uses a 20-second bloom.

**body**: Used in the context of cold brew having 'heavy body' as a characteristic some drinkers appreciate.

**boiler**: The lower chamber of the moka pot filled with water; filled just below the pressure valve as standard practice.

**bottomless design**: A structural feature of the Dwell Dripper where the brewer has no bottom, affecting how water flows through the coffee bed during brewing.

**brew cycle**: One complete heating-and-brewing operation using a set volume of water (60 ml in testing); used as the unit for measuring battery life.

**brew guide**: Refers to an included cheatsheet covering 8 brew methods with ratios, water temperature, brew times, and serving sizes.

**brew ratio**: The ratio of coffee to water; the Basic 3 can calculate and display this in real time during pour-over mode.

**brew temperature**: The temperature of the water used during extraction. The author contrasts 100°C (boiling, used by the partner) with ~93°C (the partner's preferred temperature for their own cup), implying lower temperature yields better results.

**brew time**: Total elapsed time from first water contact to end of drawdown; used as a diagnostic tool — too long means grind too fine, too short means grind too coarse. Target is ~3:30.

**brewer head**: Part of a drip/batch brewer that should be wiped with a damp cloth after cleaning to remove grounds or stains.

**brewing temperature**: Water temperature used during extraction; recommended range is 195°F–205°F (90.6°C–96°C).

**bur set**: The replaceable grinding burrs inside a manual grinder; the MHW Bomber R3 Blade is noted as upgradeable with an alternative bur set that changes the taste profile.

**burr alignment**: The accurate positioning of the two burrs relative to each other; misalignment leads to uneven particle distribution and simultaneous under- and overextraction.

**burr diameter**: The size of a grinder's outer (larger) burr measured in millimetres; a key performance indicator — larger diameters are associated with better stability and efficiency.

**burr dulling**: Gradual wearing of grinder burrs with use, which can cause color readings to drift 5–6 points over a year if the grinder is heavily used for purposes beyond color measurement.

**burr geometry**: Used in the context of the Comandante C40 Hammerhead to suggest that the shape and design of the grinding burrs can be tailored to specific coffee varieties.

**burr geometry and alignment**: Physical characteristics of grinder burrs that influence optimal grind setting; explains why the same grinder model may require different settings for different users.

**burr grinder**: A grinder that crushes coffee between two ridged metal discs or plates (burrs); grind size is set by the distance between the burrs.

**burr set**: The grinding mechanism inside the grinder; Commandante's is custom-designed and carries a lifetime warranty.

**burrs**: The ridged metal discs or plates between which coffee is ground in a burr grinder.

**bypass**: Referenced in 'zero bypass Z1,' meaning the Z1 brewer design does not route any water around the coffee bed — all water passes through the grounds.

**c price**: The commodity market price for coffee, referenced as volatile and at a 27-year high, creating financial pressure on coffee businesses.

**c40**: Model designation indicating the grinder can hold and grind up to 40 grams of beans at a time.

**cacao**: Another dominant flavor note the host identifies in Atomo's products, contributing to a vague coffee-like impression.

**calcium carbonate**: A mineral added to brew water to increase alkalinity and produce a more creamy mouthfeel.

**calibration**: Resetting the color meter to a known standard; recommended before each roast session and liberally when multiple readings fall outside the expected range.

**cappuccino**: Used as an example of a legitimate, clearly defined coffee drink the barista respects customers for ordering.

**capsule**: A sealed, pre-portioned coffee pod used in the Morning machine; specialty coffee roasters supply their own capsules with recommended brewing profiles.

**caramelisation of sugars**: A roasting process that medium roasts are said to enable, contributing to sweetness and a balanced flavour profile.

**carbon kevlar**: Composite material used in the custom build; noted as difficult to machine because kevlar frays.

**carbonate hardness (temporary hardness)**: A measurement of the water's levels of carbonates and bicarbonates, measured in ppm.

**carter everywhere mug**: A coffee product by Fellow Products, listed as a finalist in a coffee gear/product category at the 2019 Sprudgies.

**catimor**: A hybrid arabica variety crossing Caturra and Timor, used in Yunnan for its pest and coffee leaf rust resistance, high yield, and fast fruiting; described as capable of consistent 83–84 point cups but not typically reaching 87–88 points.

**catuai**: A hybrid of Mundo Novo and Caturra; one of the popular varieties grown in Malawi.

**caturra**: An arabica varietal used alongside Red Bourbon as the base varietal across all three coffees in the set.

**centre of gravity**: Referenced in the context of ergonomic cup design — a handle positioned near the cup's centre of gravity is cited as a key ergonomic parameter.

**channeling**: Uneven flow paths through the coffee puck; the reviewer claims lifting the arms before filling reduces channeling.

**channelling**: Described as something to 'fight' in the context of making true espresso; implies uneven water flow through the puck as a known challenge.

**chemex**: The pour-over brewer used in this recipe, noted for its thicker paper filter relative to other brewers.

**cherry**: The ripe coffee fruit on the tree; used to discuss harvest timing, consistency of production cycles, and quality impacts.

**chicory**: A non-coffee plant ingredient the host detects as a dominant flavor component in both Atomo products.

**citrus**: Listed alongside florals as a flavor note commonly associated with African coffees.

**clever**: Refers to the Clever Dripper, an immersion-style manual pour-over brewer; used by the author as an upgrade over a drip machine, producing the best result in their experience.

**climate-smart agricultural strategies**: Training approach for farmers designed to help them adapt to climate change and maintain or improve yields.

**clumps**: Aggregations of fine coffee particles that are visible inside the puck via CT scan; considered undesirable for espresso brewing.

**co-ferment**: A process where coffee is fermented alongside another ingredient; in the El Vergel 'Candy Blast,' the coffee is co-fermented with watermelon candy.

**co-ferment / co-fermented**: Used to describe coffees that undergo a fermentation process involving additional ingredients or organisms; the Pink Bourbon from Poem and the watermelon coffee are both described as co-ferments. The melon creamsicle blend uses 65% co-ferment combined with 35% natural Ethiopian.

**co-operative**: A collective of coffee producers (e.g., a Brazilian co-operative with 180+ members) that may seek certifications like Fairtrade to access price protections and premiums.

**co-operatives**: Farmer-owned organisations in Ghana, some community-based, used to organise smallholder coffee producers; e.g., the Sustainable Coffee Farmers Association operates five co-operatives.

**cocobod**: Ghana's regulatory body established in 1947 that oversees cocoa, coffee, and shea production, including R&D, seedling production, technical support, certification, and international marketing.

**coffee berry borer**: A pest (implied by context, name truncated in source) that has devastated harvests in Nepal in recent years.

**coffee cherry**: The fruit of the coffee tree, referenced in the context of Red Caturra harvest.

**coffee cocktails**: Beverages made at Cabin that combine coffee with cocktail techniques, with the stated goal of highlighting the full flavor range coffee can express, not just coffee-forward taste.

**coffee extraction**: The process by which flavours and aromas are extracted from coffee beans into the final brew; less extraction yields more acidity/sourness, more extraction yields more bitterness.

**coffee leaf rust**: A fungal disease that Catimor is naturally resistant to, making it an attractive variety for Yunnan growers.

**coffee maker**: Refers to a standard automatic drip coffee machine, described as 'shitty,' implying it is a limiting factor in cup quality.

**coffee refractometer**: A device used to measure TDS/extraction levels; credited with teaching baristas how brewing parameters affect extraction and helping narrow preferred parameter ranges.

**coffee rust**: A fungal disease (Hemileia vastatrix) that devastated coffee production in Brazil, Africa, Java, and eventually the Philippines in the 1880s, from which Philippine production never fully recovered.

**coffee value assessment (cva)**: The framework underpinning the new Q Grader program; moves beyond a single numerical score to consider taste, experience, and context in evaluating coffee quality.

**coffee-to-water ratio**: The proportion of coffee grounds to water used in brewing; traditional cold brew is described as at least 1:10 (coffee:water), which Hoffmann considers wasteful.

**coil preheating**: The first stage of the Lassen X's water heating system, where water is preheated in a coil before entering the boiler chamber.

**cold brew**: Coffee brewed with water that is room temperature to cold at the time of brewing; distinct from hot-brewed coffee that is subsequently chilled.

**color meter / color reader**: Device used to measure the color of roasted coffee (whole-bean or ground) as a proxy for roast level and flavor development; multiple models exist with varying sample size requirements and accuracy.

**commercial coffee**: Lower-priced, often blended coffee sold in supermarkets; associated with darker roasts and robusta content in this source.

**concentrate**: Refers to the pre-mixed Third Wave Classic stick dissolved in 1 gallon of distilled water, used as a stock solution that is then further diluted at the kettle stage.

**concentric circles**: The recommended pouring pattern — starting in the center and spiraling outward — used for both the bloom and the main pour to ensure even saturation of grounds.

**cone dripper**: A pour-over brewing device with a cone-shaped filter; contrasted with flat-bottom drippers in terms of flavor profile and flow rate.

**conical burrs**: One of two main burr shapes; described as often more affordable and quieter; often preferred by hand grinder makers.

**conical dripper**: A pour-over brewer with a cone-shaped brewing bed (e.g., Hario V60); associated by the host with flavor intensity, prominent acidity, and highlighted complexity.

**contact heat**: Heat transferred by direct contact with the roasting drum surface, as opposed to hot air; too much contact heat causes burning.

**contact time**: The duration water spends in contact with coffee grounds; Hoffmann states it is a direct function of extraction — longer contact time means higher extraction.

**corrosion**: The chemical degradation of aluminium caused by coffee acids and improper cleaning agents (vinegar, lemon juice, dishwasher), which can release metals into the brew.

**crema**: The cream-like layer produced on espresso as a result of extracting at 8–10 bar of pressure; Gaggia called it 'caffe creme.'

**crp (coffee rehabilitation program)**: A Ghanaian government program approved in 2014 covering research, extension services, and introduction of high-yield coffee varieties, supporting over 4,500 smallholder farmers.

**crust break**: The moment during cupping when the floating coffee crust is broken; Prodigal breaks the crust at 4:00 minutes.

**ct scanner (computed tomography)**: An imaging device that uses x-rays from multiple angles across a 360-degree rotation to build a 3D density map of an object; used here to examine the interior structure of espresso pucks.

**cup of excellence**: A competitive coffee quality award; one of the coffees served was described as a Cup of Excellence entry.

**cup score**: A numerical quality score given to a coffee during cupping. Prodigal's floor is 87.5 points for a coffee to enter the main product line.

**cupping**: Referenced as part of the selection process — 'months of planning, cupping, coordinating with roasters' — indicating standardized sensory evaluation of coffees.

**cupping score**: A numeric evaluation of coffee quality; the source notes that achieving a specialty-grade cupping score does not necessarily translate to financial benefits for Philippine farmers.

**dark roast**: A roast level the reviewer claims can be brewed without preheating using the new arm-lifting technique.

**decant**: Immediately pouring brewed coffee into another container after pressing to stop the brewing process and prevent over-extraction.

**defects**: Visual flaws on coffee beans (broken beans, burn marks, discoloration) used as indicators of lower quality and lower price.

**degassing valve (ventil)**: A one-way valve on coffee bags that allows CO2 released by fresh roasted coffee to escape without letting oxygen in; cited as a reason to keep coffee in its original bag.

**development**: The degree to which a coffee bean has been fully roasted through; pre-blending risks some beans being underdeveloped while others are over-developed.

**development time ratio (dtr)**: Pat targets 13% development time for both coffees in the blend to align their solubility and produce a balanced, harmonious extraction.

**dial in**: The process of adjusting brewing parameters to achieve optimal results; Rao argues limiting variables makes dialing in more efficient.

**dialed in**: Used to describe a coffee that has been precisely calibrated for optimal extraction; Sub Zero's freezing model aims to preserve a coffee in its 'dialed in' state.

**dialing in**: The iterative process of adjusting brew variables (dose, yield, time) to achieve a target recipe; used in the context of where scale responsiveness matters most.

**digital scale**: Used to measure coffee dose (36g), rinse water (60–75g), ice (270g), bloom water (~70g), and remaining brew water (235g) with precision.

**dilution**: The weakening of brewed coffee concentration by melting ice when served over ice; accounts for why cold brew recipes often use a slightly higher coffee-to-water ratio.

**direct lever machine**: Used as a comparator for the Primus paddle feel — examples given are Flair 58 and Robot — implying tactile, immediate pressure response.

**direct relationships / direct trade**: Specialty roasters building personal sourcing relationships with coffee producers, resulting in more traceable, high-quality lots.

**direct trade**: A sourcing relationship in which the roaster transacts directly with the farmer, bypassing middlemen. Rao notes these relationships exist but are not the norm, especially with certain origins or large lots.

**distribution**: How coffee grounds are arranged or spread within the portafilter basket before brewing; the experiment aims to compare different distribution methods.

**distribution shower screen**: A component mentioned as built into the portafilter setup to help distribute water evenly over the puck.

**dollarisation**: Ecuador's adoption of the US dollar as its currency, described in the context of its impact on production costs and economic stability for coffee producers.

**dosage**: The ratio of ground coffee to water; source recommends one rounded tablespoon per 4-ounce cup as a baseline.

**dose**: The amount of ground coffee placed into the basket, referenced when describing what the CT scan reveals about a single serving of coffee.

**dosing**: The process of measuring coffee grounds going into the portafilter; listed as a consistency variable.

**dosing ring**: A magnetic ring that fits over the portafilter basket to prevent coffee grounds from spilling during distribution and tamping; part of the BOOKOO Puckgo system.

**double boiler**: Boiler configuration in the La Marzocco Linea Micra that provides separate boilers for brewing and steam, enabling precise temperature control.

**down-dosing**: Using less coffee than the standard dose; recommended for coffees that produce excessive fines when a coarser grind is not achievable.

**drag and draw style**: A latte art pouring technique for which the KIMERA's #08 high precision tapered spout was specifically designed.

**draw down**: The final phase of a V60 brew after pouring is complete, where the remaining water drains through the coffee bed and paper. Hoffmann notes this phase is where many brewers observe slow flow and worry about brew quality.

**drawdown**: Implicitly described as the phase after pouring ends when water drains through the grounds; complete when the bed is dry and flat (~1 minute after pouring ends).

**drip coffee**: The poster's previous baseline brewing method, used as a comparison point for taste and caffeine strength.

**dry down**: The final stage of a pour-over brew when remaining water drains through the bed; a longer dry down is associated with the Cris Slow filter.

**drying oven**: Referenced as equipment César uses at Finca Betel and explains to visitors.

**drying patios**: Open-air surfaces used to dry coffee at Finca Las Palmas; toured during the visit.

**dual boiler**: A boiler configuration the reviewer explicitly says the Lassen X is NOT — used as a reference point to clarify the machine's hybrid system.

**dual-wall glass**: The material used for the AeroPress Premium's brewing chamber; chosen because it is the only transparent non-plastic alternative, but brings fragility and dimensional changes.

**e61 group head**: Referenced as the platform the Lelit Mara X attempts to modernize; also used as a category of machines that may offer flow control but not direct pressure control.

**ek grinder**: A specific grinder model used by Mame; one unit dedicated to espresso, another to filter.

**ek-43**: Mahlkönig's commercial electric grinder, referenced as an example of a high-end shop grinder the channel left mostly unused in favor of the Commandante.

**electronic pressure relief valve**: A valve in the Lassen X that can bleed off water to modulate pressure, enabling advanced pressure profiling.

**end testing**: A quality-control step performed after assembly but before packaging, carried out separately from the assembly process.

**espresso**: Pressure-based concentrated coffee extraction; mentioned as a valid choice when visiting a café known for quality espresso, but less preferred than filter coffee by the experts interviewed.

**espresso mode**: A dedicated scale mode featuring auto-tare and automatic shot timing triggered by the first drops of extraction.

**espresso-based beverages**: The primary drink category served at the family's high-volume café, particularly during morning and post-lunch peak hours.

**ethical accreditation**: A form of certification or labeling that signals ethical sourcing or production practices to consumers.

**even/uniform extraction**: The idea that flat bottom drippers extract coffee grounds more evenly than conical drippers; the host is skeptical of its importance and treats it as an overemphasized narrative in specialty coffee.

**exclusive lot**: A coffee produced specifically for the Pull & Pour Coffee Club, as with the SEY Peru Cusco lot.

**extended fermentation**: Used by 8th & Roast to describe a prolonged fermentation stage in the natural process, contributing to distinctive flavor development.

**extended wash**: A variation of washed processing involving longer fermentation or washing stages, referenced as a method Colombian producers are experimenting with.

**external water reservoir**: A detachable tank on the Primus that sits outside the main machine body, noted for enabling a smaller footprint and easier water monitoring and refilling.

**extract**: One of the three selectable modes on the Moka Bot's touchscreen, corresponding to the brewing/extraction phase.

**extraction**: Used to compare the degree to which coffee solubles are drawn out of the grounds; Hoffmann notes that factory papers at four minutes did not match the extraction quality of the alternative papers.

**extraction level**: The degree to which solubles are drawn from coffee grounds; higher extraction often (but not always) correlates with better results according to Rao.

**extraction pressure**: Named as a critical espresso variable alongside temperature, dosing, tamping, and distribution.

**extraction temperature**: Identified as a variable in the espresso extraction process that contributes to inconsistency when not controlled.

**extraction uniformity**: How evenly water passes through the coffee puck, improved by flow rate control.

**fairtrade minimum price**: A guaranteed floor price for Fairtrade-certified coffee that protects farmers from market price drops below a set value; buyers cannot purchase below this minimum.

**fairtrade premium**: An additional payment of US 20 cents/lb on top of the price paid for Fairtrade coffee; at least 25% must be invested in training and support for quality, productivity, and sustainability.

**farm-to-roaster**: Describes the supply chain model of the company Carl Sara works for — sourcing directly from farms through to roasting.

**faux crema**: The crema-like foam produced by the moka pot brew; the poster notes it dissipates after pouring, distinguishing it from true espresso crema.

**fellow**: Referenced as a brand of kettle that the community allegedly insists is necessary for pour over, contrasted with a cheaper $40 kettle.

**fellow ode**: An electric burr grinder used in the group-brewing setup for speed and efficiency when multiple people want coffee.

**fermentation**: The Panamanian Geisha from Jose Galdo is described as coming with fermentation, used as a distinguishing processing characteristic.

**fermentation character**: Flavor attributes introduced by the fermentation process; described in the 13-day anaerobic as present but 'intentional and developed' rather than chaotic.

**fermentation forward**: Used to describe a style of coffee processing or expression where fermentation character is prominent; the source finds 95°C unlocks these coffees well.

**filter basket**: The aluminium mesh component of the moka pot through which water is forced; can become clogged and is described as replaceable.

**filter coffee**: Used interchangeably with pour-over / brewed coffee; contrasted with espresso as a preferred brew method by multiple interviewees.

**filter mesh**: The mesh component of the French press filter; recommended to be scrubbed vigorously with a toothbrush and baking soda paste monthly for heavy users.

**filter rinse**: Pre-wetting the paper filter before brewing to eliminate paper taste and ensure the filter holds its shape in the cone.

**filter station**: A dedicated area of the bar for filter brewing, kept separate from the espresso machine area so barista workflows do not cross.

**filter3**: A filter-brewing method used by Prodigal to evaluate potential green coffee purchases from a different sensory angle than cupping.

**filtration**: The step of separating brewed liquid from coffee grounds; identified as the core problem with cold brew because cold water filters very slowly through grounds.

**finca**: Spanish term for farm/estate, used in the context of Tim Wendelboe & Elias Roas's recognized coffee farms (Finca Taman and Finca El Suelo).

**fine grind**: The grind size recommended by the seller for use with this device (15g, fine, tamped).

**fines**: Very small coffee particles produced during grinding; Hoffmann notes fines can clog filter papers, slow brew time, and contribute bitterness to the cup.

**first batch**: Prodigal's heavily discounted offering for roast batches that do not meet QC standards (color, weight loss, or cup score), sold at reduced price rather than discarded.

**first click**: The reference point for grind size adjustment — the first counterclockwise step from the zero/locked position of the grind dial.

**first crack**: A stage in the roasting process referenced as a timing benchmark — Rao wants color and weight-loss data from the previous batch before reaching first crack in the current batch.

**flash chilling**: Implied method — chilling coffee immediately as it is brewed, rather than cold brewing or chilling after the fact.

**flat bottom dripper**: A pour-over brewer with a flat brewing bed (e.g., Kalita Wave), contrasted with conical drippers; associated by the host with balanced, rounded, sweeter flavor profiles.

**flat burrs**: One of two main burr shapes; described as generally more expensive, more consistent, and prone to retaining more heat; popular with high-quality electric grinder manufacturers.

**flat white**: An espresso-based drink the author treats as a sophisticated, exacting order that separates serious coffee shops from lesser ones.

**flavor wheel**: Mame's own tool for presenting coffees to customers in an approachable, fun way — analogous to an ice cream flavor menu, letting customers choose by flavor preference before diving into origin details.

**floating**: A producer-level process of placing cherries in water to identify and remove underripe or defective ones, used to minimize quaker content before processing.

**florals**: Listed as a characteristic flavor note associated with African coffees generally.

**flow control**: The rate at which water enters the brew chamber, here determined by the size of the holes in the flow tower rather than by the brewer's hand.

**flow profiling**: The practice of varying water flow rate during extraction; the reviewer notes it is often confused with pressure profiling on E61-style machines.

**flow rate**: How quickly water drains through a filter paper; used as the primary variable distinguishing the papers tested, measurable by pouring water through a dry paper with no coffee.

**flow rate regulation**: Controlling the rate at which water flows through the coffee puck to improve extraction uniformity.

**flow resistance**: The degree to which a paper filter slows liquid passage; the Cris Slow filter intentionally increases flow resistance for a longer dry-down.

**flow restrictor**: A component removed from the Slayer to allow maximum flow rate through the secondary valve.

**flow tower**: The inner component of the Hoop that holds the coffee and filter paper, and controls water flow via sized holes around its perimeter.

**flowering season**: The period when coffee trees bloom before cherry development; the source highlights the problem of simultaneous flowering and cherry production on the same trees.

**foundational recipe**: A fixed set of brewing parameters (dose, temperature, ratio, water chemistry) kept constant across coffees; only grind changes when switching coffees.

**four-bar linkage**: A mechanical mechanism used to drop the espresso platform to a workable height from the tall Cybertruck bed.

**frankenmoka**: Hoffmann's term for a modified moka pot rig he built for earlier experimentation to develop his 'ultimate technique.'

**french press**: A coffee brewing device the poster is learning to use for the first time; associated with stronger coffee and a steeping process.

**freshly roasted beans**: Beans purchased from a local market, recommended by the community as a key upgrade over standard supermarket coffee.

**front of house**: Referenced as a management responsibility that fell to the head barista in the owners' absence.

**fruit-forward**: A descriptor for the 9-day anaerobic natural, indicating more prominent fruit flavors compared to the washed baseline.

**full-time casual**: The poster's employment classification — casual status but working full-time hours across the week.

**gasket seal**: The circular rubber seal that sits between the two halves of a moka pot; described as commonly overlooked in maintenance and in need of semi-regular replacement.

**semipermeable membrane**: The membrane through which water is forced under high pressure in a reverse osmosis system to remove dissolved minerals.

**shade grown**: Used to describe a cultivation method where trees (e.g., graviola, eucalyptus) are planted to reduce direct sun exposure on coffee trees, particularly for exotic varieties like Geisha and Wush Wush.

**shop master**: The name of an espresso machine being installed and debuted in the Brazilian market at the family's high-volume São Paulo café.

**silicone seal**: The component at the bottom of the plunger that creates a tight seal against the chamber wall during pressing.

**siphon action / siphoning**: The mechanical process in the GX2 where water is pulled through the coffee bed rapidly; the length of the siphon tube modulates how much siphoning occurs.

**siphon dripper**: A pour-over brewer mechanism (used in the GX2) where water is sucked through the coffee bed via siphon action once water reaches a certain level, accelerating drawdown.

**sl-34**: A coffee variety/cultivar name referenced in connection with the Communist States coffee brewed as a pour-over during the visit.

**slayer one group**: A single-group espresso machine referenced as a high-end, desirable machine known for its needle valve and manual flow-rate control capability.

**slow bar**: Referenced as an existing specialty coffee format involving more deliberate preparation; Cabin's model is described as going even further in hospitality than typical slow bars.

**slurry**: The mixture of coffee grounds and water during brewing; referenced in the context of a crust forming at the top that may benefit from a light stir.

**small lots**: Limited-quantity, high-quality coffee sourced by specialty roasters in the early 2000s, often better suited to filter than espresso.

**small roasters**: Used to mean specialty or independent coffee roasters, contrasted with mass-market brands like Folgers; associated with a noticeably better experience.

**smallholder coffee authority (sca)**: A government-created organisational structure for the smallholder coffee sector established in 1971; abolished in 1999 after it was found to retain 70–80% of sales proceeds from farmers.

**smallholder coffee farmer's trust (scft)**: Founded in 1999 to replace the SCA, focused on increasing coffee quality and value across the supply chain; converted into the MCPCU in 2007.

**smallholder farmers**: Coffee producers growing on less than three hectares, typically living below the poverty line; the target population for the 20/20 Project.

**smallholder producers**: Used to describe Ecuadorian farmers with just two to three hectares of coffee who must also grow other crops to sustain their operations.

**smart espresso profiler**: A device referenced as a prior product by Gabor, the maker of the Roastino; mentioned to establish the maker's credibility.

**solubility**: The ease with which coffee compounds dissolve during extraction; described as key to excellent espresso, favouring more developed roast profiles over very light roasts.

**solubility matching**: The now-questioned practice of blending only coffees with similar extraction yields (solubility); Rao argues the end result approximates blending brewed cups and was never fully valid as a strict rule.

**soluble coffee**: Instant/soluble coffee production that historically used large volumes of lower-quality Yunnan coffee, contributing to the region's low-quality reputation.

**special release**: Refers to exclusive or limited coffee drops from the Pull & Pour Coffee Club, distinct from regular club offerings.

**specialty coffee**: Used broadly to mean coffee produced and handled with care at every stage of the supply chain; also used to denote coffees of the quality level seen at barista/brewers cup championships.

**specialty grade**: A quality classification requiring coffee to be defect-free and have notable qualities; the source notes farmers must pay for evaluation to achieve this grade.

**specialty instant coffee**: Carried as a backup or insurance option in the minimal kit, not as a primary brew preference.

**specialty-grade**: Used as a quality benchmark that many Malawian estate coffees are described as not yet consistently reaching.

**split shot**: Dividing a double espresso output into two separate cups; used at Sensory Lab to make their version of a flat white.

**spoon method**: A tasting technique where spoonfuls from individual cupping bowls are combined in various ratios into empty cups, then tasted blind, to determine optimal blend ratios.

**spread (outer vs. inner roast color)**: The difference between the color of the bean's outer surface and its interior (ground color); measuring both whole-bean and ground color helps roasters learn to manipulate this spread.

**spring lever machine**: A type of espresso machine where the user pulls a lever to engage a spring mechanism that drives water through the puck; described as the dominant machine type in Naples (La San Marco lever).

**sprudgie awards**: Sprudge's annual awards recognizing excellence across multiple categories in the specialty coffee world, including cafés, roasters, producers, writers, educators, and social media.

**squeegee scrape**: A distinctive mechanical step in the Clover's brewing process, eventually assigned to the barista rather than automated.

**standalone model**: A puck press that operates independently, not attached to or integrated with a grinder.

**steamed milk**: Milk that has been heated and textured with steam; referenced as what a customer who wants very little coffee flavor and mostly milk should simply order.

**steaming milk**: A feature of the bean-to-cup DeLonghi that enables milk-based drinks.

**steep**: The process of immersing coffee grounds in water and allowing them to sit for an extended period during cold brew production.

**steep / infusion test**: A test where filter papers are bundled, submerged in hot water, and steeped for 5–10 minutes; the resulting water is tasted to detect flavor differences between paper types.

**steep time**: A programmable parameter on the Clover controlling how long the coffee grounds are immersed before being separated from the water.

**steeping**: The stage of French press brewing where grounds sit in hot water; the poster learned that stirring during this stage is recommended.

**structure**: Used to describe the organized, articulate quality of Kenyan coffees and increasingly of African coffees broadly.

**sub zero (concept)**: A coffee service model where coffees are frozen after being dialed in, preserving them for later service — potentially days, weeks, or months later.

**sudan rume**: A coffee varietal grown at Inmaculada farm in Colombia, roasted by Corvus.

**super-automatic espresso machine**: A machine that carries out most steps in espresso and milk preparation automatically, reducing the manual input required from the barista.

**sustainability**: Used critically in the article as a widely applied but poorly defined buzzword; the source argues its meaning must be co-defined with farmers rather than imposed from high-income country perspectives.

**sustainable sourcing**: A sourcing approach that accounts for social, economic, and environmental factors to support farmers and ensure long-term supply chain viability.

**sweetness**: Cited as an emerging quality in African coffees that has improved alongside structure and consistency.

**tamp**: The seller's instructions specify that the 15g dose should be tamped, applying espresso-style preparation to the moka pot basket.

**tamping**: Pressing down the ground coffee sample in the measurement tray to create a smooth, flat surface, which improves color measurement accuracy.

**tare**: The act of zeroing the scale to account for the weight of a container or portafilter; on the Basic 3, activated by a single tap of the touch controls.

**tds**: Total Dissolved Solids, measured as a percentage using a refractometer; used as the primary metric for espresso strength throughout the video. Standard espresso range cited as 8–12% TDS.

**tds (total dissolved solids)**: Used as a measure of espresso concentration; Perger targets ~9% TDS for milk drinks and notes longer shots reduce TDS.

**temperature probe**: A sensor placed in the bottom of the Moka Bot brewer that measures water temperature and passes data back to the controller.

**terroir**: Used in the context of the Finca Potosí natural lot to suggest that the farm's environment contributes to the coffee's character alongside processing.

**thermal block**: A component at the group head of the Primus that fine-tunes water temperature for the shot, working in conjunction with the boiler.

**thermal extraction**: Pat uses 96°C water to create a 'higher thermal extraction' to intensify the aroma of the Gesha variety.

**thermal stability**: The ability of the device to maintain consistent brewing temperature; the reviewer suggests the basket's sheltered position in the Orbit aids thermal stability.

**thermo 3d double-wall**: MiiR's proprietary double-wall stainless steel construction technology used in the New Standard Carafe to keep coffee hot longer.

**third wave**: Used historically to describe the movement that added intentionality to coffee creation/craft, contrasted with the next phase of adding intentionality to service and hospitality.

**third wave coffee**: A movement in the coffee industry characterised by craft brewing, world championships, and heightened focus on quality and origin.

**tipping**: A roasting defect where a small part of the bean — specifically the soft part — gets slightly burned; described as common in lighter roasts.

**toddy brewer**: A specific brand of cold brew brewer using a two-step filtration system (thin paper bag + cloth filter); used as an example of traditional cold brew equipment.

**tomography**: The technique of imaging cross-sections through an object; the 'tomography' part of CT refers to these cross-sectional slices.

**topography**: The physical terrain of the farm used deliberately to influence shading levels and sun exposure for different coffee plots.

**total dissolved solids (tds)**: Used interchangeably with minerals removed by RO filtration; a measure of the mineral content of water.

**traceability**: The ability to track coffee's origin through the supply chain; cited as a deficiency in much Philippine coffee.

**training centers**: Used in reference to Counter Culture Coffee's network of educational locations across the United States, recognized in an education-related Sprudgie category.

**varietal**: Used broadly to refer to the genetic variety of the coffee plant (e.g., Gesha, Pink Bourbon, Sudan Rume); the Nestor Lasso set featured four unique varietals.

**variety**: A sub-category within a coffee species (e.g., Geisha, Bourbon, Pacamara within Arabica), each with distinct flavour characteristics.
