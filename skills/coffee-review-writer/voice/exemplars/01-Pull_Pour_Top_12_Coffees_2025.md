2025 has been another big year in coffee. Over the past twelve months, hundreds of coffees from roasters all over the world have been brewed—spanning dozens of origins, processing styles, and varietals. Here are the standout coffees listed in close to the order they were tasted:

---

1. HACHI-KONDORU WASHED GEISHA
Roaster: NATIVE
Country: Colombia
Notes: This was a beautiful washed Geisha from Diego Bermundez from his Hachi Project. It had wonderful notes of melon, mandarin, and florals. A really stunning example of the Geisha varietal.

---

2. COLOMBIA MONTEBLANCO
Roaster: Poem Roasting
Country: Colombia
Notes: A washed co-fermented Pink Bourbon from roaster Poem. Notes of watermelon Jolly Ranchers, cantaloupe & peach rings. One of the more fun coffees of the year.

---

3. YEMEN BANI MATAR NATURAL
Roaster: Pull & Pour Special Release
Country: Yemen
Notes: One of the special release drops of the Pull & Pour Coffee Club. Had notes of rich chocolate and dark fruit like pomegranate paired with leathery sweet tobacco notes—unique, complex & delicious.

---

4. MELON CREAMSICLE
Roaster: Colorfull
Country: Colombia / Ethiopia
Notes: This coffee made the list for being one of the most unique coffees ever tasted. A blend pairing 65% of a co-ferment with 35% of a natural Ethiopian bringing a more dynamic and layered flavor profile. The final result was exactly as the name described—a melon creamsicle.

---

5. 96 HR ANAEROBIC HONEY GEISHA
Roaster: Gregoron Coffee
Country: Colombia
Notes: The flavor call of "Trix cereal" on this coffee encompassed the sweet fruitiness that explodes in the second half of each sip. Wonderfully delicate with layered jasmine/green tea component, drinking more like a washed or honey processed coffee than a long fermentation anaerobic.

---

6. EUDIS OROSCO
Roaster: SEY
Country: Peru
Notes: An exclusive lot for the coffee club. Part of an entirely new initiative for SEY in the Cusco region. Peru coffees usually don't make the top list, but this one drank much more like Pink Bourbon lots from Colombia with a unique Gesha-like sweetness and florality.

---

7. NESTOR LASSO VARIETY SET
Roaster: Goshen
Country: Colombia
Notes: Technically a set of four coffees from the same producer, Nestor Lasso. The set consisted of four unique varietals, all harvested simultaneously and processed using the same anaerobic washed method. Each coffee was uniquely delicious, making it one of the most fun and tastiest coffee experiences of the year.

---

8. COLOMBIA SIERRA MORENA PINK BOURBON
Roaster: Wonderstate
Country: Colombia
Notes: Pink Bourbon is one of the favorite varietals. This one from Wonderstate was a favorite of the year. Bright, juicy, and intensely layered, it led with tangy red currant and zesty citrus, finishing with depth of stone fruit and sweetness reminiscent of Demerara sugar.

---

9. WATERMELON CO-FERMENT
Roaster: Coffee Man
Country: Colombia
Notes: This coffee struck the perfect balance of watermelon flavors without being overly processed or fake-tasting. It was the perfect summer coffee, enjoyed iced all summer long.

---

10. INMACULADA NATURAL SUDAN RUME
Roaster: Corvus
Country: Colombia
Notes: Inmaculada is one of the top farms in Colombia and their Sudan Rume is some of the best coffee they produce. This coffee was a treat from Corvus with strong, complex notes of vanilla, mango and tropical fruit.

---

11. ETHIOPIA ALO WASHED
Roaster: Blendin
Country: Ethiopia
Notes: Part of the Pull & Pour 12 Days of Coffee Christmas set. Came from favorite Ethiopian producer, Tamiru Tadesse. Delicate and complex with floral notes of peach.

---

12. ETHIOPIA RIVER ANAEROBIC
Roaster: Aviary
Country: Ethiopia
Notes: Also part of the 12 Days of Coffee Christmas set. Came from favorite roaster, Aviary. Has very unique processing and equally unique flavor profile with notes of hibiscus, blue raspberry, melon & plum. One of favorite coffees as the year ended.

---

The past year was full of genuinely memorable cups, and it was lucky to have spent so much time with them. Here's to discovering even more standout coffees and continuing the journey, one brew at a time.
