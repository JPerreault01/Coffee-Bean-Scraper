PULL & POUR'S TOP 12 COFFEES FROM 2025
Source: pullandpourcoffee.com | Author: Andrew Pautler | Date: December 31, 2025

OVERVIEW
Over 2025, Andrew Pautler of Pull & Pour brewed hundreds of coffees from roasters worldwide. The following 12 stood out most, listed roughly in the order tasted.

---

1. HACHI-KONDORU WASHED GESHA — NATIVE
   Country: Colombia
   A washed Gesha from producer Diego Bermundez's Hachi Project. Notes of melon, mandarin, and florals.

2. COLOMBIA MONTEBLANCO — POEM ROASTING
   Country: Colombia
   Washed co-fermented Pink Bourbon. Notes of watermelon Jolly Ranchers, cantaloupe, and peach rings. Standout from a newly discovered roaster.

3. YEMEN BANI MATAR NATURAL — PULL & POUR SPECIAL RELEASE
   Country: Yemen
   Part of a Yemen-focused special release series for the Pull & Pour Coffee Club. Notes of rich chocolate, dark fruit (pomegranate), and leathery sweet tobacco.

4. MELON CREAMSICLE — COLORFULL
   Country: Colombia / Ethiopia (blend)
   65% co-ferment blended with 35% natural Ethiopian. Described as literally tasting like a melon creamsicle — one of the most unique coffees tried.

5. 96 HR ANAEROBIC HONEY GEISHA — GREGORON COFFEE
   Country: Colombia
   Flavor notes of Trix cereal and sweet fruitiness; surprisingly delicate with jasmine/green tea, drinking more like a washed coffee than a long-ferment anaerobic.

6. EUDIS OROSCO — SEY
   Country: Peru
   An exclusive lot from SEY's new initiative in the Cusco region. Drank like a Pink Bourbon from Colombia, with Gesha-like sweetness and florality. Unusual for a Peru offering.

7. NESTOR LASSO VARIETY SET — GOSHEN
   Country: Colombia
   A set of four coffees from world-renowned producer Nestor Lasso, all harvested simultaneously and processed via anaerobic washed. Only the varietal differed across the four. Unique and delicious.

8. COLOMBIA SIERRA MORENA PINK BOURBON — WONDERSTATE
   Country: Colombia
   Bright, juicy, and layered with tangy red currant, zesty citrus, stone fruit, and Demerara sugar sweetness. One of Andrew's favorite varietals.

9. WATERMELON CO-FERMENT — COFFEE MAN
   Country: Colombia
   Struck the perfect balance of watermelon flavor without feeling processed or artificial. Enjoyed iced all summer.

10. INMACULADA NATURAL SUDAN RUME — CORVUS
    Country: Colombia
    From Inmaculada, one of Colombia's top farms. Strong, complex notes of vanilla, mango, and tropical fruit.

11. ETHIOPIA ALO WASHED — BLENDIN
    Country: Ethiopia
    From producer Tamiru Tadesse; part of the Pull & Pour 12 Days of Coffee Christmas set. Delicate and complex with floral notes of peach. Described as the style Andrew would drink forever if he had to choose one.

12. ETHIOPIA RIVER ANAEROBIC — AVIARY
    Country: Ethiopia
    Also from the 12 Days of Coffee Christmas set. Unique processing yields notes of hibiscus, blue raspberry, melon, and plum. One of the year's final favorites.

---
NOTES
- Co-ferments were heavily represented this year, reflecting a broader trend in specialty coffee.
- Yemen and Ethiopia continued to be standout origins.
- Pink Bourbon varietal appeared multiple times as a top performer.
