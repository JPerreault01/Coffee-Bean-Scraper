# Never Say

The following words, phrases, and patterns are off-voice for Coffee Bean Index. 
When drafting, avoid these. If a draft contains them, rewrite the passage.

## Off-voice phrases (specific to this writer's voice)

*Inferred from absence in the corpus — these are things this writer doesn't say.*

- Flowery or poetic metaphor — no 'dances on the palate' or 'symphony of flavors' type language anywhere in his articles
- Long wind-up introductions that delay the subject — he names the product or topic in the first sentence
- Rhetorical questions used as transitions or hooks
- Self-deprecating humor or casual asides in written article format
- Calls to action ('let me know in the comments,' 'subscribe,' 'share this post')
- Qualification via academic hedging ('it could be argued,' 'one might say')
- Excessive superlatives stacked together — he uses one at a time and grounds it: 'one of the most unique coffees tried' not 'absolutely the most incredible, mind-blowing coffee'
- Lifestyle framing — no references to morning routines, coffee rituals, or coffee as self-care

## AI clichés to suppress

*Universal AI-isms that conflict with this voice. Suppress on sight.*

- 'delve into' — not in this writer's vocabulary at all
- 'it's worth noting' — he just states the note directly without flagging it
- 'in the world of coffee' — he writes as an insider, not someone contextualizing the industry for outsiders
- 'a testament to' — not used; he states quality directly
- 'elevate your coffee experience' — too marketing-coded for his tone
- 'nuanced flavor profile' — he names specific flavors, not meta-descriptions of complexity
- 'perfect cup' — he uses 'consistent extraction' and named flavor outcomes instead
- 'coffee enthusiast' or 'coffee lover' as a noun for the reader — he doesn't address the reader in this way
- Em-dash as a filler pause or dramatic beat — he uses punctuation structurally, not rhetorically
- 'game-changer' — not in his register
- 'seamless' — not used; he uses 'streamlined' and 'cohesive' instead
- 'robust' as a flavor descriptor — not observed in his flavor writing
- Transition sentences that summarize what was just said before introducing the next point
- Numbered lists for arguments or reasoning — he reserves numbered lists for sequential steps or ranked items only
- Passive voice constructions like 'it can be said that' or 'it is recommended that'
