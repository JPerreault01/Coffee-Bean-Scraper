# Voice Profile
*Extracted from Jackson's writing samples.*

## Tone

Jackson P's writing is matter-of-fact and list-driven, with enthusiasm expressed through concise declarative statements rather than effusive prose. A representative phrase: 'Described as literally tasting like a melon creamsicle — one of the most unique coffees tried.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt). The tone is that of a knowledgeable enthusiast who trusts the reader to keep up, not a teacher explaining from scratch.

## Sentence patterns

**Length preference:** Short to medium. Most sentences in the articles are one to two clauses. Longer sentences appear when providing context or listing attributes, but rarely exceed 30 words. Evidence: the top coffees article uses almost exclusively 1-2 sentence entries per coffee.

**Structure:** Subject-verb-object is dominant. Descriptive entries often lead with a category label or noun phrase before the predicate (e.g., 'Notes of melon, mandarin, and florals.'). Standalone noun phrases and fragments are common in list-format writing.

**Rhythm:** Punchy and declarative. Short complete sentences alternate with noun-phrase fragments. Lists do heavy structural lifting, which creates a staccato rhythm. No meandering subordinate clauses.

**Examples of typical sentences:**
- Notes of rich chocolate, dark fruit (pomegranate), and leathery sweet tobacco. (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- Streamlined workflow from distribution through tamping. (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- A polished, well-engineered puck prep kit. (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)

## Vocabulary signature

Words and phrases this writer uses distinctively. Reach for these.

- 'standout' — used to flag a notable discovery: 'Standout from a newly discovered roaster.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- 'co-ferment' / 'co-fermented' — used as both noun and adjective without explanation, assuming reader familiarity: '65% co-ferment blended with 35% natural Ethiopian.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- 'washed' as a standalone process descriptor — never 'wet-processed': 'Washed co-fermented Pink Bourbon.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- 'anaerobic washed' — specific hybrid process term: 'processed via anaerobic washed.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- 'layered' — used for flavor complexity: 'Bright, juicy, and layered with tangy red currant.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- 'juicy' — a key positive flavor descriptor: 'Bright, juicy, and layered.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- 'florals' (plural noun) — shorthand for floral notes: 'Notes of melon, mandarin, and florals.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- 'delicate' — used for light-bodied or subtle coffees: 'surprisingly delicate with jasmine/green tea.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- 'polished' — used as quality shorthand for gear: 'A polished, well-engineered puck prep kit.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- 'cohesive' — used to describe system-level design quality: 'creates a unified workflow rather than three standalone items.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- 'caveats' — used explicitly in verdicts: 'Rating: Recommended with caveats on price.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- 'channeling' — used as a technical espresso term without definition: 'preventing channeling.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- 'repeatability' — used as a technical brewing value: 'Calibrated tamper improves repeatability.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- 'dialing in' / 'dial in' — standard specialty coffee verb, used naturally: 'once the workflow is established.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- 'special release' — used as a product category term: 'Part of a Yemen-focused special release series.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)

## Stance patterns

Pautler states opinions directly and without hedging when it comes to personal taste ('one of the most unique coffees tried,' 'the style Andrew would drink forever if he had to choose one'). On gear and value, he qualifies his recommendations explicitly, naming the target audience and the conditions under which his verdict applies ('Not ideal for those who already own a WDT tool, distributor, and tamper they're happy with'). He does not soften negative assessments with excessive politeness — shortcomings get their own labeled section.

## Opening patterns

- State the product or subject immediately, then give the single most important framing fact. Example: 'The Timemore PUCKS Espresso Accessories Set is a bundled trio of puck prep tools designed to bring consistency to home espresso.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- Overview block before detail — use a brief paragraph to orient the reader before any list or section. Example: 'Over 2025, Andrew Pautler of  brewed hundreds of coffees from roasters worldwide. The following 12 stood out most, listed roughly in the order tasted.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- Lead with a bolded section header ('OVERVIEW') followed by a short orienting paragraph, before any structured data. (01_top_coffees_2025.txt, 02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)

## Closing patterns

- Named verdict section ('OVERALL VERDICT') that summarizes who the product is for and who should skip it, ending with a concise rating line. Example: 'Rating: Recommended with caveats on price.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- End with a 'NOTES' section that surfaces meta-observations about broader trends or patterns seen across the reviewed items. Example: 'Co-ferments were heavily represented this year, reflecting a broader trend in specialty coffee.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- Closing does not trail off — the last line is a concrete, standalone statement, not a call to action or a rhetorical question.

## Transition patterns

- Section headers do the transitional work — the writing does not use connective prose to move between major topics. Headers like 'WHAT WORKS WELL' and 'SHORTCOMINGS' replace transitional sentences. (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- Within a section, bullet points replace transitional sentences entirely for lists of features or attributes. (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- When a new coffee entry begins in a list, the entry number and bolded name serve as the transition — no bridging prose. (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- Occasional short contextual parenthetical to add detail without a new sentence: 'processed via anaerobic washed. Only the varietal differed across the four.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)

## Specific tics

*Quirks the writer probably doesn't notice but that mark the voice.*

- Em-dash used sparingly but precisely to add a clarifying detail mid-sentence: 'Wide, rounded grips; WDT tool handle doubles as a magnetic storage stand.' — actually uses semicolons more than em-dashes in lists. (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- Parenthetical asides for secondary detail inside list items: 'dark fruit (pomegranate)' and 'jasmine/green tea.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- Forward slash used to join two closely related items rather than writing 'and': 'jasmine/green tea,' 'black or wood finishes.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt, 02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- Named sections in ALL CAPS as structural dividers: 'OVERVIEW,' 'WHAT'S IN THE BOX,' 'WHAT WORKS WELL,' 'SHORTCOMINGS,' 'OVERALL VERDICT.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- Numbered and bulleted lists used heavily to carry descriptive content — prose paragraphs are short and few. (01_top_coffees_2025.txt, 02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- Country listed as a labeled data field per coffee entry, not embedded in prose: 'Country: Colombia.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- Price anchored with a tilde for approximation: '~$199 retail.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
- Flavor notes presented as a comma-separated list, not as a descriptive sentence: 'Notes of melon, mandarin, and florals.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)
- Third-person self-reference in his own articles: 'One of Andrew's favorite varietals.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)

## Topic stances

**co-fermented coffees**: Enthusiastic and treats them as a legitimate, exciting trend — not a gimmick. 'Co-ferments were heavily represented this year, reflecting a broader trend in specialty coffee.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)

**Pink Bourbon varietal**: Explicitly one of his favorites: 'One of Andrew's favorite varietals.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)

**Yemen and Ethiopia as origins**: Consistently described as standout origins: 'Yemen and Ethiopia continued to be standout origins.' (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)

**Washed Gesha style**: Described as the style he'd 'drink forever if he had to choose one' in reference to the Ethiopia Alo Washed. (01_top_coffees_2025.txt)

**Bundled espresso prep kits (like Timemore PUCKS)**: Positive but conditional — recommends with price caveats and notes the category isn't novel: 'Not a new concept — bundled espresso prep kits have existed for years.' (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)

**Gear value**: Explicitly audience-segmented — he names who should buy and who should skip, rather than making a universal recommendation. (02_timemore_pucks_review.txt)
